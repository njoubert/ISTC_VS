GCafe Talk: Resolution Loss without Blur: Recovery Limits in Pointwise Degradation

Yoav Schechner
Monday, Dec. 14, 2.30pm
Gates 392

Pointwise image formation models appear in a variety of photography, vision and image-based rendering problems, such as: lens glare, microscopic fluorescence mixtures, specular/diffuse reflection, flash faloff, backscatter and attenuation in media, direct/indirect illumination, and more. An expanding array of methods has been devised to handle such problems. However, what is the recovery limit? Are there object features that cannot be effectively recovered under a pointwise degradation, despite the best efforts made by the recovery method? If some objects are not recovered, is there a point in trying to develop a new method to salvage them, or is their loss fundamental? How does this depend on the object/feature size? We derive bounds to recovery from pointwise degradations. The bounds apply even if the parameters of a recovery algorithm are perfectly set. The analysis uses a physical model for the acquired signal and noise, and also accounts for potential post-acquisition n oise filtering. The analysis yields an effective cutoff-frequency, which is induced by noise, despite having no optical blur in the imaging model. We show the consequence to real applications.
